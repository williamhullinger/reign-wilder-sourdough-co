A Better Place to Start

You do not need a giant bakery setup.

Great sourdough at home starts with a handful of dependable tools. A good scale, a reliable starter jar, a bench scraper, a proofing setup, and a solid baking vessel will take you much farther than a crowded drawer full of gadgets.

These are the core tools that show up again and again in sourdough baking. If someone only wanted the essentials, this is the group we would point them to first.

Most Important

Digital Kitchen Scale

If you buy one thing first, make it a scale that reads in grams. It makes starter feeding, hydration, and recipe consistency much easier.

  • Measures in grams
  • Easy to tare between ingredients
  • Helpful for starter and dough accuracy
View Recommended Scale Why grams matter

Starter Care

Starter Jar Kit

A straight-sided starter jar with a level marker and temperature strip makes it much easier to watch rise, timing, and overall starter health.

  • Rise marker or feeding band
  • Temp strip for better consistency
  • Easy to clean and easy to read
View Starter Jar Options

Daily Use

Bench Scraper

One of the simplest and most useful tools in bread baking. Great for lifting dough, dividing portions, shaping, and keeping the counter clean.

  • Useful for sticky dough
  • Helps with shaping and cleanup
  • Works for bread, bagels, and focaccia
View Bench Scrapers

Proofing

Proofing Basket

A banneton helps dough keep its shape during final proofing and gives that classic flour-dusted artisan look.

  • Supports shape during proofing
  • Round or oval options
  • Often includes a liner
View Proofing Baskets

Scoring

Bread Lame

A bread lame gives you cleaner scoring, better control over oven spring, and a more polished finished loaf.

  • Cleaner cuts before baking
  • Replaceable blades
  • Helpful for classic sourdough scoring
View Bread Lames

Baking

Dutch Oven

A Dutch oven traps steam early in the bake, which helps with crust, color, and oven spring. It is one of the most common home-baker setups for artisan sourdough.

  • Great for crust development
  • Beginner-friendly baking setup
  • Works well for round artisan loaves
